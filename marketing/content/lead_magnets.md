# The Complete Identity Verification Compliance Checklist

## Introduction

Identity verification is no longer just a fraud prevention measure‚Äîit's a regulatory requirement. From KYC (Know Your Customer) to AML (Anti-Money Laundering) to data privacy regulations, businesses face an increasingly complex compliance landscape.

This checklist will help you ensure your identity verification process meets regulatory standards and protects your business from compliance violations.

---

## Section 1: KYC (Know Your Customer) Requirements

### Customer Identification Program (CIP)

- [ ] Collect full legal name
- [ ] Collect date of birth
- [ ] Collect residential or business address
- [ ] Collect identification number (SSN, passport, tax ID)
- [ ] Verify identity within a reasonable time after account opening
- [ ] Document the verification methods used
- [ ] Retain records for at least 5 years after account closure

### Identity Document Verification

- [ ] Verify government-issued photo ID
- [ ] Check document authenticity (security features)
- [ ] Verify document hasn't expired
- [ ] Match photo to applicant
- [ ] Verify document hasn't been reported lost/stolen
- [ ] Check for signs of tampering or forgery
- [ ] Cross-reference with issuing authority when possible

### Risk Assessment

- [ ] Assign risk rating to each customer (low, medium, high)
- [ ] Apply enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers
- [ ] Document risk assessment methodology
- [ ] Review and update risk ratings periodically
- [ ] Consider geographic risk factors
- [ ] Consider business type risk factors
- [ ] Consider transaction pattern risks

---

## Section 2: AML (Anti-Money Laundering) Compliance

### Suspicious Activity Monitoring

- [ ] Monitor transactions for suspicious patterns
- [ ] Establish thresholds for suspicious activity reports (SARs)
- [ ] Train staff to recognize red flags
- [ ] Document suspicious activity investigation procedures
- [ ] File SARs within required timeframes (typically 30 days)
- [ ] Maintain confidentiality of SAR filings
- [ ] Retain SAR records for 5 years

### Watchlist Screening

- [ ] Screen against OFAC SDN list
- [ ] Screen against other sanctions lists (UN, EU, HMT)
- [ ] Screen against PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) lists
- [ ] Screen against adverse media
- [ ] Implement real-time screening for transactions
- [ ] Document screening procedures and results
- [ ] Establish escalation procedures for matches

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

- [ ] Understand the nature of customer's business
- [ ] Understand expected transaction patterns
- [ ] Identify beneficial owners (for business accounts)
- [ ] Verify source of funds when necessary
- [ ] Update customer information periodically
- [ ] Document all due diligence efforts
- [ ] Apply enhanced due diligence for high-risk relationships

---

## Section 3: Data Privacy Compliance

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

- [ ] Obtain explicit consent for data processing
- [ ] Provide clear privacy notices
- [ ] Implement data minimization practices
- [ ] Ensure data accuracy and currency
- [ ] Provide data subject access rights
- [ ] Establish data retention schedules
- [ ] Implement data deletion procedures
- [ ] Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
- [ ] Appoint Data Protection Officer (if required)
- [ ] Document lawful basis for processing

### CCPA/CPRA (California Privacy Laws)

- [ ] Provide "Notice at Collection"
- [ ] Honor consumer rights requests
- [ ] Provide "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link
- [ ] Maintain data inventory
- [ ] Train staff on consumer rights
- [ ] Update privacy policy for CCPA requirements
- [ ] Implement verification procedures for consumer requests
- [ ] Establish service provider agreements

### Data Security

- [ ] Encrypt data in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- [ ] Encrypt data at rest (AES-256)
- [ ] Implement access controls
- [ ] Maintain audit logs of data access
- [ ] Conduct regular security assessments
- [ ] Have incident response procedures
- [ ] Train employees on data security
- [ ] Use secure data disposal methods

---

## Section 4: Industry-Specific Requirements

### Fintech/Banking

- [ ] Comply with BSA (Bank Secrecy Act) requirements
- [ ] Implement FinCEN regulations
- [ ] Follow FFIEC guidelines
- [ ] Meet PCI DSS standards (if handling cards)
- [ ] Comply with state money transmitter laws
- [ ] Register with FinCEN as required
- [ ] Maintain BSA/AML compliance program

### Healthcare

- [ ] Comply with HIPAA requirements
- [ ] Implement Business Associate Agreements
- [ ] Conduct HIPAA risk assessments
- [ ] Train staff on HIPAA compliance
- [ ] Implement minimum necessary standard
- [ ] Maintain audit logs for 6 years
- [ ] Report breaches within required timeframes

### Cryptocurrency/Blockchain

- [ ] Comply with FinCEN's 2013 guidance
- [ ] Implement Travel Rule compliance (FATF)
- [ ] Register as MSB (Money Services Business)
- [ ] Maintain transaction records
- [ ] Report suspicious transactions
- [ ] Comply with state-specific crypto regulations
- [ ] Implement blockchain analytics

### E-commerce/Marketplaces

- [ ] Verify seller identities
- [ ] Monitor for fraudulent listings
- [ ] Implement buyer protection policies
- [ ] Screen for prohibited products/services
- [ ] Maintain transaction records
- [ ] Cooperate with law enforcement
- [ ] Implement chargeback prevention

## Section 6: Red Flags and Warning Signs

### Document Red Flags

- [ ] Document appears altered or forged
- [ ] Photo doesn't match applicant
- [ ] Document expired or not yet valid
- [ ] Security features missing or incorrect
- [ ] Information inconsistencies across documents
- [ ] Document reported lost or stolen
- [ ] Unusual wear patterns for claimed age

### Behavioral Red Flags

- [ ] Customer reluctant to provide information
- [ ] Customer provides false or suspicious information
- [ ] Unusual transaction patterns
- [ ] Transactions inconsistent with stated business
- [ ] Multiple accounts with similar information
- [ ] Use of shell companies or complex structures
- [ ] Frequent changes to account information

### Geographic Red Flags

- [ ] Customer from high-risk jurisdiction
- [ ] Transactions to/from high-risk countries
- [ ] Use of offshore accounts without clear purpose
- [ ] Transactions crossing multiple jurisdictions rapidly
- [ ] Customer in different location than claimed

## Section 1: Meeting Agenda Templates

### Template 1: Weekly Team Standup

**Purpose:** Quick sync on progress, blockers, and priorities

**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Agenda:**
1. Wins from last week (2 min)
2. Priorities for this week (5 min)
   - Each team member: 30 seconds
3. Blockers that need help (5 min)
4. Announcements (3 min)

**Rules:**
- Standing meeting (literally)
- Parking lot for long discussions
- Start on time, end on time

## Section 2: Pre-Meeting Preparation Checklist

### For Meeting Organizers

- [ ] Define clear meeting purpose and desired outcomes
- [ ] Identify required vs. optional attendees
- [ ] Create and share agenda 24 hours in advance
- [ ] Include pre-reading materials with agenda
- [ ] Book appropriate room/video conference
- [ ] Send calendar invites with agenda attached
- [ ] Prepare any necessary materials or demos
- [ ] Set up collaboration tools (Miro, FigJam, etc.)
- [ ] Assign roles (facilitator, note-taker, timekeeper)
- [ ] Estimate and allocate time for each agenda item

### For Meeting Attendees

- [ ] Review agenda and pre-reading materials
- [ ] Prepare updates or input relevant to agenda items
- [ ] Come with questions or concerns
- [ ] Arrive/join 2 minutes early
- [ ] Close unnecessary applications/tabs
- [ ] Test audio/video if joining remotely
- [ ] Come prepared to make decisions

## Section 7: Best Practices Guide

### The 10 Rules of Productive Meetings

**1. No Agenda, No Meeting**
If there's no clear agenda with desired outcomes, cancel the meeting.

**2. Start and End on Time**
Respect everyone's time. If you're late, you miss out.

**3. Invite the Minimum Necessary People**
Every attendee should be essential. Observers can read notes.

**4. Assign Roles**
Every meeting needs a facilitator, note-taker, and timekeeper.

**5. No Devices (Unless Note-Taking)**
Phones and laptops distract. Be present or be elsewhere.

**6. Park Long Discussions**
If a topic needs more time, schedule a separate meeting.

**7. End with Clear Action Items**
Every meeting should produce decisions and assigned tasks.

**8. Share Notes Within 24 Hours**
Memories fade. Get the summary out while it's fresh.

**9. Track Action Items**
Follow up on commitments. Accountability drives results.

**10. Regularly Audit Your Meetings**
Cancel recurring meetings that have lost their purpose.
